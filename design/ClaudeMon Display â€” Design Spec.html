Gradients

avoid — see note

RGB565 caveat: the panel stores 5 bits red, 6 green, 5 blue — only 32 / 64 / 32 levels per channel, not 256. Subtle shades collapse and smooth gradients band visibly. Design with flat fills and clearly-separated tones; avoid low-contrast gradients, soft glows, and near-identical grays. If a value looks slightly off on-device vs. your mockup, that's the 565 rounding.

Interaction

Touch — single finger only

Tap

Primary input. Keep interactive targets ≥ 44×44 px. Buttons should look tappable (fill/border).

Horizontal swipe

Flips pages instantly (no animated slide — see performance). Left = next, right = prev.

No multi-touch

The controller is single-touch: no pinch, no two-finger gestures. Don't design for them.

Feedback

State changes should be instant + visible (color/opacity), not animated transitions.

Performance

What keeps it smooth

The RGB panel continuously scans its framebuffer out of PSRAM, which is bandwidth-tight. Big animated repaints starve that scan and stutter — so the visual language is static and instant, not animated.

Do

  • Flat fills, crisp edges, static layouts
  • Instant page/state switches (show/hide)
  • Small charts & bars that update on data pushes (~every few min)
  • Dark backgrounds; reserve bright fills for small accents
  • Design for a screen that may auto-dim at night

Avoid

  • Full-screen slide / fade / zoom transitions
  • Anything re-painting large areas every frame
  • Smooth gradients & soft glows (band in RGB565)
  • Continuous motion, spinners over big regions, video
  • Large solid blocks that change color frequently
